INGRAINED: Wood in a Cross-Section of Time, Koel Gallery

Exhibition — Koel Gallery Karachi

Wood has been my muse for two decades. This exhibition is a love letter to the trees that have carried our stories far longer than we have walked among them.

At the generous invitation of Noor Jehan Bilgrami at Koel Gallery, curating this show alongside my dear friend Naila Mahmood was a treasured experience. It taught me, moved me, and changed how I think about my own practice. Ingrained is a celebration of wood and a tribute to the incredible participating artists whose imagination and hands have brought it alive.

Curatorial Statement - An Invitation To Listen To The Wood

Living Witness

As living witnesses, trees mirror our personal and collective histories. The exhibition celebrates how they hold the weight of national identities, cultural memory, and the struggle over land and ecology. As we read wood, we in essence enter a dialogue with time — layered, resilient, and profoundly alive.

Tethered To Trees

The exhibition explores the intricate bond between humans and trees — a bond we have too often betrayed, felling forests without reverence and denying the sanctity with which they hold our ecosystems, our histories, and our very existence in balance.

Rooted In Time

In the architecture of wood, time quietly reveals itself. Trees carry the memory of growth, rupture, and renewal within their bodies. The exhibition explores how trees are more than material; they are metaphors of rootedness and resilience, of belonging and displacement, of innovation and legacy.

The Subject & The Medium

A vessel of texture, grain, and warmth, inviting touch and transformation in the artisans hands. As they carve, join, turn and finish, they read the wood's memory, responding with awareness and understanding.
